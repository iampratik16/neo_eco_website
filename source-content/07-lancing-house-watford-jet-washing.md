# The Case for Planned Cleaning: Jet Washing at Lancing House, Watford

The cheapest communal cleaning is the one you do regularly. Left too long, dirt, moss and algae work their way deep into surfaces and turn a routine wash into a major restoration. At Lancing House on The Reeds in Watford, our **planned maintenance jet washing** kept the communal areas in good order before any of that could happen. This case study was prepared for Ms Jennifer Mann, Senior Property Manager at Rendall & Rittner Ltd.

## Why Regular Beats Reactive

We have seen first-hand what happens when communal jet washing is neglected for years: deep-rooted weed growth, blocked drains, lost joint sand and multi-day restoration projects. **Preventative pressure washing** avoids all of that. By cleaning communal areas on a regular schedule, build-up never gets the chance to establish, the work stays quick and straightforward, and the surfaces last longer. Planned maintenance is always cheaper than restoration.

## The Work at Lancing House

We cleaned the everyday communal surfaces: the **entrance, the steps and the path leading to them**, using a thorough three-stage method:

1. **Manual scrubbing** to lift ingrained dirt and any overgrown mould.
2. A **light chemical treatment** to clear biological staining.
3. A **strong jet wash** to flush the surface back to a clean finish.

Even on a maintained block, this staged approach delivers a far better and longer-lasting result than a quick rinse.

## The Result

The before and after pictures show a clear uplift across the communal areas at Lancing House, brighter, cleaner surfaces that residents can enjoy through spring and summer. Keeping the block on a regular cleaning rhythm is what keeps these results consistent year after year.

## Building Jet Washing Into Your Maintenance Plan

For managing agents, the smartest move is to schedule communal **jet washing** as a recurring line in the annual maintenance plan rather than calling it in only when surfaces look bad. A planned **annual jet washing schedule** keeps presentation consistent, spreads cost predictably, and protects surfaces from the kind of deep neglect that leads to expensive restoration.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**How often should communal areas be jet washed?**
For most blocks, once a year is the baseline, with shaded or high-traffic areas benefiting from more frequent visits.

**Is a maintenance contract cheaper than one-off cleans?**
Regular scheduled cleaning is almost always more cost-effective. It prevents heavy build-up, keeps each visit efficient, and avoids costly restoration work later.

**Can you set up a recurring cleaning schedule?**
Yes. We work with managing agents to build communal cleaning into a planned maintenance programme.
