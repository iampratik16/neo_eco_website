# Service Charge Well Spent: Jet Washing at Wellington House, Watford

Leaseholders want to see their service charge working. Few maintenance jobs make that as visible as communal **jet washing**, where the difference is plain to anyone walking through the door. At Wellington House on The Reeds in Watford, our team delivered exactly that kind of visible value across the communal areas. This case study was prepared for Ms Jennifer Mann, Senior Property Manager at Rendall & Rittner Ltd.

## Visible Value Matters

Much of what a service charge pays for is invisible, insurance, reserves, compliance, the things residents rarely see. **Communal cleaning** is the opposite. When the entrance, steps and paths are bright and well kept, every resident sees their money at work each time they come home. That visibility is exactly why **block cleaning** is one of the most reassuring lines on any service charge budget.

## The Work We Carried Out

We cleaned the **entrance, the steps and the path leading to them**, the areas residents use and judge daily. The job followed a three-stage method:

1. **Manual scrubbing** to lift ingrained dirt and overgrown mould.
2. A **light chemical treatment** to clear biological staining cleanly.
3. A **strong jet wash** to flush the surface back to a clean finish.

## The Result

The before and after pictures show a clear, visible uplift across the communal areas at Wellington House. Brighter, cleaner surfaces that residents notice immediately and enjoy through the spring and summer, the kind of result that quietly justifies the spend.

## Helping Managing Agents Demonstrate Value

For managing agents, a clean block is one of the easiest ways to show leaseholders that their service charge is being managed well. Before and after photographs from each visit, like the ones in this report, give agents a simple, tangible record to share with residents and demonstrate **value for money**. It builds trust and reduces complaints.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Is communal jet washing good value on a service charge budget?**
Yes. It is highly visible, relatively low cost, and directly improves resident satisfaction, making it one of the better-value maintenance lines.
