# First Impressions Count: Entrance Jet Washing at Badminton House, Watford

The entrance is the first thing a resident, visitor or prospective buyer sees, and it sets the tone for the entire building. At Badminton House on The Reeds in Watford, our team gave the communal entrance a thorough **jet washing** to restore that all-important first impression. This case study was prepared for Ms Jennifer Mann, Senior Property Manager at Rendall & Rittner Ltd.

## The Brief: Restore the Communal Entrance

We focused on the areas residents use every single day: the **communal entrance**, the steps, and the path leading up to them. These high-traffic zones collect dirt, algae and grime faster than anywhere else on the property, which is exactly why **communal entrance cleaning** has such an outsized effect on how the whole block feels.

## How We Did It

A strong result on weathered entrance surfaces takes more than pointing a lance and walking away. Our process at Badminton House combined three stages:

1. **Manual scrubbing** to break down ingrained dirt and overgrown mould in the worst-affected areas.
2. A **light chemical treatment** to lift biological staining without harsh, environment-damaging products.
3. A **strong jet wash** to flush everything away and reveal the surface beneath.

This staged approach is what separates a genuine **pressure washing** restoration from a quick rinse that looks the same a fortnight later.

## The Result

The before and after pictures speak for themselves. The entrance, steps and path at Badminton House were given a significant lift, brighter, cleaner and far more welcoming. It is the kind of uplift residents notice the moment they walk in, and one they will enjoy through the brighter spring and summer months.

## Why Entrance Cleaning Matters for Managing Agents

For a managing agent, the communal entrance is a shop window. A clean, well-kept entrance signals a well-run building, reassures leaseholders that their service charge is being spent well, and supports property values. A neglected one does the opposite. Regular **entrance jet washing** is one of the highest-impact, lowest-cost ways to keep a block looking cared for.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**How often should a communal entrance be jet washed?**
Once or twice a year keeps entrance steps and paths free of algae and grime. Shaded, north-facing entrances often need more frequent attention.

**Will jet washing make the steps slippery?**
The opposite. Removing algae and mould restores grip and reduces slip risk on communal steps.

**Do you work around residents?**
Yes. We schedule **communal entrance cleaning** to keep access safe and minimise disruption.
