# Safer Steps for Every Resident: Communal Stair Jet Washing at Westminster House, Watford

Communal steps are used dozens of times a day, by residents of every age and mobility. When they are coated in grime and algae, they become a slip hazard. At Westminster House on The Reeds in Watford, our **communal stair cleaning** restored safe, clean footing across the entrance and steps. This case study was prepared for Ms Jennifer Mann, Senior Property Manager at Rendall & Rittner Ltd.

## Why Step Safety Comes First

Slips and trips on communal stairs are among the most common accidents in residential blocks, and the consequences can be serious. A build-up of algae, mould and dirt on steps creates a slick surface that becomes especially treacherous when wet. For freeholders and managing agents, keeping communal steps clean is part of a clear duty of care. **Step jet washing** removes that slip risk and restores grip.

## The Work We Carried Out

We focused on the **entrance, the steps and the path leading to them**, the surfaces most critical to safe access. The job followed a three-stage method:

1. **Manual scrubbing** to lift ingrained dirt and break up overgrown mould on the steps.
2. A **light chemical treatment** to clear algae and biological staining.
3. A **strong jet wash** to flush the surface clean and restore traction.

## The Result

The before and after pictures show a clear improvement. The steps and entrance at Westminster House were left clean, bright and, crucially, safer underfoot, giving residents confident access through the wetter months and a brighter look heading into spring and summer.

## Keeping Communal Stairs Safe Year-Round

Step safety is not a one-off. Algae and mould return fastest in the damp, shaded spots where communal stairs often sit. Scheduling regular **stairwell and step cleaning** keeps traction consistent throughout the year and reduces the risk of accidents, protecting both residents and the managing agent's responsibilities.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Does jet washing make communal steps safer?**
Yes. Removing algae, mould and grime restores the natural grip of the step surface and reduces slip risk.

**How often should communal steps be cleaned?**
At least once a year for most blocks. Shaded or north-facing steps that grow algae quickly may need more frequent cleaning.

**Are your methods safe for residents?**
Yes. We use eco-friendly products and natural methods wherever possible, and steps are safe to use once dry.
