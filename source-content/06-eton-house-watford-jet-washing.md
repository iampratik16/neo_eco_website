# Summer-Ready in a Day: Spring Jet Washing at Eton House, Watford

After a long, damp winter, communal areas need a refresh. Months of rain, fallen leaves and low light leave entrances and paths dull, green and tired. A well-timed **spring jet washing** is the fastest way to get a block looking bright and welcoming again before summer. At Eton House on The Reeds in Watford, that is exactly what our team delivered. This case study was prepared for Ms Jennifer Mann, Senior Property Manager at Rendall & Rittner Ltd.

## Why Spring Is the Right Time

Winter is hard on outdoor communal surfaces. Damp, shade and cold create perfect conditions for algae, moss and grime to build up across steps and paths. By spring, that build-up is at its worst, which makes it the ideal moment for **seasonal pressure washing**. Cleaning in spring resets the surfaces just as residents start spending more time outdoors and natural light returns.

## The Refresh We Carried Out

We focused on the everyday communal areas: the **entrance, the steps and the path leading up to them**. The work followed a three-stage method for a finish that lasts through the season:

1. **Manual scrubbing** to lift winter dirt and overgrown mould.
2. A **light chemical treatment** to clear algae and biological staining.
3. A **strong jet wash** to flush the surface clean and bright.

## The Result

The before and after pictures show a real seasonal uplift. The communal areas at Eton House were left noticeably brighter and cleaner, ready for residents to enjoy through the spring and summer months ahead.

## Building Seasonal Cleaning Into the Plan

A single spring jet wash makes a dramatic difference, but the smartest approach is to plan it in. Scheduling **seasonal pressure washing** each spring keeps communal areas consistently presentable, prevents heavy build-up from ever taking hold, and means the block always looks its best when residents are most likely to be outside.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**When is the best time to jet wash communal areas?**
Spring is ideal, clearing winter build-up just as outdoor use increases. Some blocks add an autumn visit to manage leaf fall and damp.

**How long does a communal spring clean take?**
Most entrance, step and path areas can be refreshed in a single visit, with minimal disruption to residents.

**Do you use eco-friendly methods?**
Yes. We use natural products and eco-conscious methods wherever possible, safe for residents, pets and nearby planting.
